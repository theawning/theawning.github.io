The Awning

MAD AI

by Awn

There is a concept from the atomic era known as mutually assured destruction, rightfully abbreviated as MAD. It is the idea that two nuclear powers must never use their weapons against each other for fear of retaliation, yet must ever expand their arsenals to guarantee second strike capability. It is an insane extension of the old competition for state security that is finally beginning to break down.

With this framework, I argued in favor of artificial intelligence (AI) back in 2017, when I was still in high school. Like today, AI was an exciting and controversial technology. Unlike today, AI was a distant phenomenon. Occasionally there was some news or a report but the general public had little or no access. Still, everyone was sure of the immense economic and destructive potential of AI, which to me seemed similar to that of atomic energy. You must forgive me for I knew even at the time that it was a simple and extreme argument. However, I had a half page limit and knew what would fit. Here is what I wrote:

We Can't Afford To Not Research AI

The question of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is similar to that of human genetic modification, which I have already addressed in this class. The answer is therefore essentially the same. Just as I argued that human genetic modification must be pursued because the alternative is for someone else to develop it first, so I would argue for the development of AI. The fact is, given the wide dissemination of information, and accessibility of computing technology in our modern society, stopping someone from attempting to develop AI technology would be extremely difficult if not outright impossible. The one thing worse than having murderous AI is someone else having murderous AI while you do not. The need to develop a technology before one's rivals affects even the most anti-AI tech giants such as Elon Musk, who despite AI misgivings nevertheless has created a company to research AI.

Opponents of AI might argue that preventing AI development is an issue humanity can't afford to compromise on. A single failure could result in annihilation by a superintelligent hostile AI entity without any possible retaliation regardless of whether that AI was created deliberately or not. But again, this brings up the original point. If someone else is making (and someone else will be making) potentially dangerous AI that humans can not outwit, then logically those who oppose AI must attempt to make their own human-friendly AI to protect themselves from any potential hostile AI. Yes, this is playing with fire, but remember: Humans didn't become the dominant species in order to safeguard their existence without playing with fire, and those ancient human species that didn't play with fire are long dead and gone.
